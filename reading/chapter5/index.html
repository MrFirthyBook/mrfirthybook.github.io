			Reading List: Chapter 5: Deaf & Hard of Hearing

For easy access, here are the links to additional resources discussed in Chapter five of 'Practical Web Accessibility'. These links will provide supplementary information and learning from what's covered in the chapter, allowing you to deepen your understanding in this area.

Reading List

Practical example — WebVTT

This example includes a video with styled WebVTT captions, a sample file for you to download to write your own captions and subtitles, and an example of how to handle adding captions to an audio component using the <video> tag.

Guide to caption formats

A breakdown of the most popular caption files, and which formats are used by sites like YouTube, Vimeo, Netflix and More.

W3C - Making Audio and Video Media Accessible

This resource helps you figure out which accessibility aspects your specific audio or video needs, provides project management guidance, and includes requirements from the WCAG standard.

W3C - WebVTT Validator

A simple site where you can paste your WebVTT captions in to see if they are valid or not.

HappyScribe - Convert SRT to VTT

A free online tool to convert SubRip subtitle files to WebVTT. They also have a tool to convert the other way (VTT to SRT).

SignVideo - BSL Calls

One of the companies capable of helping those with hearing difficulties (and companies) through a video relay service.
