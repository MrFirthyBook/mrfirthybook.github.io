			Reading List: Chapter 9: Accessible Email

For easy access, here are the links to additional resources discussed in Chapter nine of 'Practical Web Accessibility'. These links will provide supplementary information and learning from what's covered in the chapter, allowing you to deepen your understanding in this area.

Reading List

Campaign Monitor - CSS Support Table

A great, free resource they maintain that shows the level of support for CSS features and approaches in all major email clients.

YouTube - Email without role="presentation"

A short video by Email on Acid, showcasing how a screen reader reads out content in a table when a role attribute of "presentation" isn't set.

YouTube - Email with role="presentation"

A short video by Email on Acid, showcasing how a screen reader reads out content in a table when you've set a role attribute of "presentation". This allows screen readers to ignore the tables and just read out the content.

Email on Acid - Accessibility Tool

An overview of how the accessibility tool works inside the email testing tool 'Email on Acid'.

Litmus - Accessible Email Made Easy

A breakdown of the accessibility checks that the email testing tool Litmus runs on your email when you use their testing platform.

W3C - PDF Technology Notes

The W3C maintain a guide around how accessibility works with PDF files, including how they're built and various user agents.

Google - Register an action button

A guide from Google around how to register to implement action buttons into your emails. There are certain criteria you need to fulfil before you're allowed to display them for Google Mail users.

Google - Test an action button schema

A handy tool from Google that allows you to test the schema of your action button, to ensure that it behaves that way that you want it to.
