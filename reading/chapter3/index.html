			Reading List: Chapter 3: Low Vision & Colour Blindness

For easy access, here are the links to additional resources discussed in Chapter three of 'Practical Web Accessibility'. These links will provide supplementary information and learning from what's covered in the chapter, allowing you to deepen your understanding in this area.

Reading List

Practical example — theme switcher

A basic example of how to create a theme toggle component on your website by making use of the CSS filter styling rule. The example stores your selection, and listens to your device settings to determine whether you prefer dark mode when you first arrive.

A Complete Guide to Dark Mode on the Web

Created by CSS tricks. It guides you through design considerations, handling user agent styles, and more.

Transport for London — Accessibility guides and maps

A range of guides to help you plan and make journeys. You can download guides below or follow the links to order printed copies, free of charge.

The Guide To Windows High Contrast Mode

Made by Smashing Magazine. Features a good set of practices you can use for that purpose, and use of the media query forced-colors and its toolset to tweak our website as needed.

Sim Daltonism — The colour blindness simulator

Sim Daltonism lets you visualise colours as they are perceived with various types of colour blindness. Use the camera on your iOS device, or use the Mac app to filter a region of the screen.

OpenDyslexic: A typeface for Dyslexia

OpenDyslexic is a typeface designed against some common symptoms of dyslexia. The newest version now uses the SIL-OFL license, giving you freedom to use it for personal use.

Dyslexie font

Dyslexie font is a typeface — specially designed for people with dyslexia — which enhances the ease of reading and comprehension.

Web.dev - Guide to responsive web design

How to create sites which respond to the needs and capabilities of the device they are viewed on.
