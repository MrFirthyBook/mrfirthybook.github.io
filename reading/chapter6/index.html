			Reading List: Chapter 6: Cognitive Impairments

For easy access, here are the links to additional resources discussed in Chapter six of 'Practical Web Accessibility'. These links will provide supplementary information and learning from what's covered in the chapter, allowing you to deepen your understanding in this area.

Reading List

Practical Example - Accessible Autocomplete

The practical example for this chapter. It showcases the difference between 'strict' and 'fuzzy' searching in an autocomplete feature, so you can add an error-tolerant version into your sites.

COGA - Making Content Usable for People with Cognitive and Learning Disabilities

The full document from the Cognitive and Learning Disabilities Accessibility Task Force, around making Content Usable for People with Cognitive and Learning Disabilities.

Readable - Reading ease

An online test to check how easy it is for someone to read your content, based on the Flesch-Kincaid reading ease test. Simply input the content and have it tested.

Hemingway editor

This editor highlights parts of your text that it deems hard to read for users, and can help you make it easier. It provides a readability grade, shows different colours to represent different issues, and even has an AI feature to suggest edits.

5,000 most common words

A list of the 5,000 base words we learn as children, to the point where we're able to recognise their shapes rather than the words themselves. These are the easiest for people to read.

Reading time calculator

Input your text into this website in order to calculate how long the average person would take to read it. It takes into account word count, length of words, and length of sentences.
