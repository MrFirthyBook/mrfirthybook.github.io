			Reading List: Chapter 4: Motor Impairments

For easy access, here are the links to additional resources discussed in Chapter four of 'Practical Web Accessibility'. These links will provide supplementary information and learning from what's covered in the chapter, allowing you to deepen your understanding in this area.

Reading List

Practical example - Accessible modal

The practical example for this chapter. It displays an accessible modal that can be controlled purely using a keyboard, can move and retain focus within itself, and returns the users keyboard focus back to where it was when they opened the modal.

Todd Stabelfeldt - WWDC iOS Switch Control Demo

This is an excerpt of Todd Stabelfeldt's keynote from Apple's WWDC 2017. In it, he showcases how he navigates an iPhone using a switch device.

Example of Mousegrid in Dragon Professional

Brian Norton, Director of AT, shows how to use the mouse grid within Dragon Professional.

Setting undo settings in Google Mail

A guide from Google on how to extend the time that the "undo email send" box appears for in Google Mail.

Guide to autofill

This guide from Dev.to covers how browsers handle autofill, how to hide it for fields like setting new passwords, and how to make it accommodating for one-time passwords (OTP's).
