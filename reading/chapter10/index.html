			Reading List: Chapter 10: Outsourcing Accessbility

For easy access, here are the links to additional resources discussed in Chapter ten of 'Practical Web Accessibility'. These links will provide supplementary information and learning from what's covered in the chapter, allowing you to deepen your understanding in this area.

Reading List

accessiBe's 'accessWidget'

To view for yourself what it feels like to use it. You can start it by interacting with the icon in the bottom right hand corner of the browser window.

Adrian Roselli — #accessiBe Will Get You Sued

Adrian's brilliant and in-depth analysis of the history of accessiBe (and tools like it) and their practices.

WebAIM Wave browser extension

The extension used to view a11y issues with a page. It's also the extension that had to add clarifying messages about accessibility overlays that attempt to fake results when their tool is run.

Apology from Shir Ekerling (CEO of accessiBe)

The full apology for accessiBe's response to feedback from the accessibility community, and its approach to selling its product as lawsuit-avoidance rather than aiming for inclusivity on the web.

Wix — Accessibility Wizard

A deep dive into Wix's 'Accessibility Wizard' tool. It's by far the most comprehensive tool I found in my research into the 'DIY Website' platforms.

MUI — Material UI-based Pattern

One the most popular free pattern libraries on the web today. It is downloaded millions of times a week and have a very active community as well as several full-time maintainers.
